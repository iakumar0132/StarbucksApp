ABOUT US

  • Starbucks was founded in 1971 by Jerry Baldwin, Zev Siegl, and Gordon Bowker at Seattle's Pike Place Market.
  • During the early 1980s, they sold the company to Howard Schultz who—after a business trip to Milan, Italy—decided to convert the coffee bean store into a coffee shop serving espresso-based drinks.
  • Starbucks believes in the pursuit of doing good.
  • Starbucks' aspiration is to be people-positive, investing in the humanity and well-being of everyone we connect with, from our partners to coffee farmers to the customers in our stores and beyond.
  • The Canadian Starbucks story began in Vancouver, BC on March 1, 1987, with the opening of the Seabus Waterfront SkyTrain location.
  • Starbucks offers a wide variety of coffee blends and beverages, including Peppermint Mocha, Caramel Latte, and the new Iced Gingerbread Oat Chai.
  • We are committed to sustainability and ethical sourcing. Our coffee is sourced through responsible practices, and we aim to reduce our environmental footprint by implementing recycling and eco-friendly initiatives in our stores worldwide.
  • Starbucks also offers a range of delicious pastries, sandwiches, and snacks, making it a perfect place for a quick breakfast or a relaxing afternoon break.
  • We take pride in our friendly baristas who are passionate about crafting the perfect cup of coffee for every customer, ensuring a warm and welcoming atmosphere in every Starbucks store.
